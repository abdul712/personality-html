1. Embrace Open Body Language

Embracing open body language is a pivotal first step in appearing more approachable and engaging in social interactions. This fundamental aspect of extroverted behavior encompasses facing the person you are interacting with directly, maintaining steady eye contact, and adopting a relaxed, confident posture.

It's essential to avoid behaviors that suggest closed-off attitudes, such as crossing arms, fidgeting, or looking away, as they can hinder the development of rapport and make others feel disconnected. Instead, implementing open body language, complemented by natural gestures and genuine smiling, signals interest, engagement, confidence, warmth, and receptivity.
